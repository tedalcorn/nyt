About

This dashboard draws on every article published in The New York Times from January 2014 to the present, retrieved through the NYT Archive API. Data refreshes automatically on the 2nd of each month.

Primary section

Each author is assigned a "primary section" based on the section in which they have the most published articles. This is a rough proxy for their beat and may not reflect their actual desk assignment, especially for authors who write across sections.

Beat topics

The topics shown on author profiles are derived from the NYT's own subject tags on each article. Topics are scored by how disproportionately an author covers them relative to the full corpus — a topic must appear at least twice as often in an author's work as the corpus-wide average to qualify. Authors with few articles or articles lacking subject tags may have no beats listed. Subject tags are assigned by the NYT, not by this dashboard.

Author names

Author name variants (e.g., "Jonah E. Bromwich" and "Jonah Bromwich") are merged when they share first and last names with compatible middle names. Only minor variants are merged; if two forms each have substantial article counts, they are kept separate to avoid false merges. Institutional bylines (The Associated Press, The Editorial Board, Reuters, etc.) are excluded from author rankings.

Geographic data

World and U.S. state coverage is based on the glocations keyword field in the API. City-level tags (e.g., "Chicago (Ill)") are mapped to their parent states. This field was discontinued in the API in 2025, so geographic data covers 2014–2024 only. Per-capita figures use 2025 population estimates.

Section names

"Fashion & Style" and "Fashion" have been merged into "Style," reflecting the NYT's own renaming of this section around 2018–2020. Sports coverage declined sharply after the NYT acquired The Athletic in 2022; Athletic content is published on a separate platform (nytimes.com/athletic/) and is not included in the Archive API, which explains the drop in Sports article counts.

Other notes

  • The current calendar year is excluded from trend charts to avoid incomplete data skewing results.
  • Word counts reflect what the API returns; some article types (interactives, briefings, live updates) may have unusual counts.
  • Article counts include all document types returned by the API (news, opinion, reviews, obituaries, etc.).
